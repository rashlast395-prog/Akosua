Aurora Studio — Brand Identity

The brief

Aurora Studio is a small Accra-based creative studio with strong work but no consistent visual identity. They were using a mismatched logo, three different fonts and colors picked per project. They needed a single, flexible system they could apply themselves across print and social.

My mandate: build a brand identity that feels premium, works in motion, and is easy for a non-designer to use day to day.

"We had the talent, but we looked like five different companies. We needed one face that people would remember."

Discovery & research

Audience & competitors

I interviewed the two founders and reviewed 14 competitor studios. Three things stood out: the market favored either cold/minimal or loud/playful — nobody owned "warm but premium". That became the positioning.

  • Audience: local brands and international clients wanting African-led creative
  • Competitors skewed minimal-neutral or maximalist-loud
  • The gap: a confident, earthy, human identity

Design direction

From the research I set three principles:

Memorable

One strong idea carried through every touchpoint so the brand is recognizable instantly.

Flexible

A system with clear rules but room to adapt across print, social and motion.

Motion-ready

Shapes and palette chosen so the logo and assets animate cleanly in video and reels.

Key design decisions

An earthy, premium palette

The old brand used whatever color fit the brief. I built a fixed palette — warm clay, deep forest and soft cream — so every post and print piece feels related at a glance.

A serif-led type pairing

A confident serif for headlines gives warmth and authority; a clean sans for body keeps things legible. The pairing reads "premium" without being cold.

Motion-ready shapes

The monogram's geometry was chosen so it animates cleanly. The launch reel used the same palette and shapes, making the brand feel alive the moment it appears.

Results

  • Social engagement: +38% in the first month after rollout
  • SKUs launched: 8 products shipped with consistent packaging
  • Team autonomy: the studio now posts without a designer each week
  • Recognition: a single, recognizable identity across every channel

"Kumi gave us one face we're proud of. We finally look like the studio we always were inside."

Reflections

The biggest lesson: a brand system is only as good as its guidelines. The templates and 12-page guide are what made this stick — without them, the team would have drifted back to old habits within a month.

If I were to do it again, I'd include motion templates from day one rather than teaching them later. The reel was the moment the brand truly clicked, and we could have shipped it faster.
